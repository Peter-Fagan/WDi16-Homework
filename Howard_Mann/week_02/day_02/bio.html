Biography

A Serious Bio

Richard Matthew Stallman is a software developer and software freedom activist.

He worked at the Artificial Intelligence Lab at MIT from 1971 to 1984, learning operating system development by doing it. He wrote the first extensible Emacs text editor there in 1976, and developed the AI technique of truth maintenance.

In 1983 Stallman announced the project to develop the GNU (GNU's not Unix) operating system, a Unix-like operating system meant to be entirely free software. In 1991, the Linux kernel appeared (developed by Linus Torvalds) and combined together with the operating system developed by the GNU project allowed for the first operating system that was free, known as Linux.

Stallman started the Free Software Foundation, campaigning against software patents and extension of copyright laws.

As of 2016, Stallman has received fifteen honorary doctorates and professorships.
